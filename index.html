Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about True Vine Mission Academy, the Spellers Method, and how to get involved.

The Spellers Method is an evidence-based approach to communication that presumes competence in nonspeaking individuals. Rather than assuming limited intelligence due to the inability to speak, the Spellers Method teaches students to communicate by spelling out words and sentences — unlocking the voices and intellect that have always been present. It is grounded in the understanding that motor challenges, not cognitive limitations, are what prevent many nonspeaking individuals from communicating.

Spelling is designed for individuals with motor-sensory challenges that prevent them from producing reliable or effective speech. All communication requires motor skills — the inability to speak does not mean a person has nothing to say or does not understand the world around them.

At True Vine Mission Academy, we welcome students who are nonspeaking, minimally speaking, or whose speech is unreliable. We believe the barrier is motor, not intellectual — and every voice deserves to be heard.

True Vine Mission Academy is designed for nonspeaking and minimally speaking students who have historically been placed in "life-skills" programs rather than given access to grade-level academic instruction. We serve students with autism, motor differences, and other conditions that affect verbal communication — students whose intelligence has been underestimated or overlooked by traditional special education systems.

True Vine Mission Academy is set to open in February 2027 in Celina, TX — located in the greater DFW area of North Texas. We are currently in the process of identifying our permanent 14-acre campus, which will be a nature-integrated facility purpose-built for the sensory and academic needs of our students. Stay connected via our Facebook page for updates on our campus search and launch timeline.
